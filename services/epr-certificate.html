EPR (E-WASTE MANAGEMENT) CERTIFICATE

Introduction

EPR stands for Extended Producer Responsibility. It is a registration for the Indian manufacturer / importer of the E-waste management material. EPR Authorisation is issued by the Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) under MoEFCC, Government of India. With the authorisation of the EPR, the manufacturer / importer of electronic and electrical goods has been given the responsibility to monitor the E-waste of the products at the end of their lifespan.

How to get EPR Registration?

The manufacturer / importer of electronic and electrical goods must provide documentary evidence of an E-waste management program and arrangement with the Recycling Agency.

Validity and Renewal

EPR Authorization is valid for 5 years and it can be renewed subsequently. Few documents and fees will be needed to get renewal.

Registration Process

Process of EPR Authorization is divided into following steps:

Step-1: Application with required enclosures

Step-2: Review of Application by CPCB

Step-3: Submission of additional information, if required by CPCB

Step-4: Grant of Certificate by CPCB

Post CPCB Approval

Please note the following:

  • CPCB, after grant of EPR Authorization, shall forward the EPR Plan to respective State Pollution Control Board for monitoring.
  • The concerned State Pollution Control Board shall monitor the compliance of EPR Authorization and submitted plan, take cognizance of any non-compliance and inform CPCB for taking action, if necessary.
  • CPCB may, also, conduct random checks.
  • EPR Authorized unit is required to maintain necessary records related to e-Waste management and file annual returns.
  • CPCB shall conduct random sampling of electrical and electronic equipment placed on the market and verify the compliance of RoHS provisions as per its guidelines and the cost for sample and testing shall be borne by EPR Authorized organization.
