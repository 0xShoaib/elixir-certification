ISO CERTIFICATION

Introduction

The International Organization for Standardization is an internationally independent body that set various global standards for quality management. This consists of national standardization bodies from different countries. Established in 1947, the ISO establishes and promotes industrial and commercial standards worldwide.

Which ISO Certification best for my business?

There are different types of ISO certification standards that focus on different aspects of business or organizational activity. ISO 9001 focuses on a quality management system that helps the organization to increase customer satisfaction with the companyâ€™s products and services. Likewise, ISO 14001 focuses on environmental management and so on.

Until gaining ISO certification, you will take a thorough look at the entire business system and evaluate that part or parts of the process you want to standardize with ISO standards. Choose an ISO standard accordingly. Below the broad category, give you context on different ISO standards at a glance and help you choose the right ISO certification for your company.

  • QMS: ISO 9001:2015 (Quality Management Systems)
  • EMS: ISO 14001:2015 (Environmental Management Systems)
  • OHSAS: OHSAS 18001:2007 (Occupational Health & Safety Management Systems)
  • ITSMS: ISO 20000-1:2011 (Information Technology Service Management Systems)
  • FSMS: ISO 22000:2018 (Food Safety Management System)
  • ISMS: ISO 27001:2013 (Information Security Management Systems)
  • EnMS: ISO 50001:2018 (Energy Management System)
  • MDQMS: ISO 13485:2016 (Medical Device Quality Management Systems)
  • OHSMS: ISO 45001:2018 (Occupational Health and Safety Management System)
  • FSSC 22000
